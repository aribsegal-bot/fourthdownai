The 3 Predictive Pillars of the Rookie Engine

1. YPTPA (Yards Per Team Pass Attempt)

The ultimate efficiency metric. We take a player's receiving yards and divide it by the total pass attempts their college team made while they were on the field. It proves whether a player was the engine of the offense. Elite Threshold: >3.0

2. Dominator Rating

The percentage of their team's total receiving yards and touchdowns the player accounted for. If you can't dominate college cornerbacks, you won't dominate the NFL. Elite Threshold: >30%

3. Breakout Age

The exact age a player first achieved a 20%+ Dominator Rating. Players who break out at 18 or 19 years old have vastly superior NFL hit rates compared to late-bloomers who beat up on younger players when they were 22. Elite Threshold: <19.5 Years Old
